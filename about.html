About

Hey! My name is Icarus, and I'm currently based in the Californian central valley. I was brought up in a small town north of LA, but otherwise haven't been around much. My sights are set on joining a career in the field of history or anthropology! I found my way into front-end design through sheer curiosity of the subject, and I just happened to be lucky enough to have access to an amazing program offered in collaboration with my local high school. Besides goals and plans and all that fun stuff, I'm also a huge nerd. I'm super into new-age Final Fantasy games (specifically the Nova Crysallis trilogy), other assorted JRPGs (Gravity Rush, NiER: Automata), animanga, and DC's Batman branch. I grew up surrounded by Japanese media since my parents had me soon after moving back to the U.S. from three years abroad there, so it's a huge part of my current lifestyle. Outside of my usual geeking I also enjoy a good read, and I also partake in indoor rock-climbing!

Education

(2013-2025)

I've attended Clovis East High School since my first year in high school, but I also began attending the Center for Advanced Research and Technology during my third year. During this time, I've taken AP Psychology, Web Applications, and Honors English 11.


Future

I plan to major in anthropology. I'll most likely minor in something related like linguistics. I hope to eventually work up to a masters!


Experience

During my second year of high school, I became the social media manager of my high school's GSA club. When I held this position, it was my responsibility to brainstorm ideas for events, plan them, volunteer at them, and manage our presence online. I also participated in the Web Applications class provided at the Center for Advanced Research and Technology during my third year. This class has taught me to effectively use HTML, CSS, Github, and a basic amount of JS. On a less professional level, I've also taken art and design commissions for close friends of mine.


Interests

Subject-wise, I've been in love with learning about clinical psychology ever since 7th grade. I especially take interest in personality and dissociative disorders. I used to be a big space geek, but I'm not incredibly devoted to that now (my favorite kind of star is a neutron star, Pluto is a planet because I say so, and Io is my favorite moon, if you were wondering). I'm also incredibly fascinated by photography and cinematography, especially moody environments like Blade Runner 2077 and Wes Anderson/Wong Kar-wai styles of color grading. I've looked into a lot of career paths revolving these subjects, but in the end I want to find myself in a field where I can help connect people, no matter what I end up pursuing.
