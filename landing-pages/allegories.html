Allegories

Culture & Society Development

Early Feb 2023 - Current

Collaboration with Halo Veare

A collection of redesigns for the main characters I have designed in Allegories so far. Their outfits mainly consist of naturally obtained dyes and fabrics.

Intro

Allegories itself isn't my project—I am actually a collaborator alongside the actual creator: Halo Veare. Veare began this project to address his religious trauma and the journey it takes to escape the only life you've known all along. My closeness to him allowed me to take the honour of adding my own touches to his project and developing the antagonists as well as their society.

Story Outline

Vague is a young man whose society had taught him to destroy an alien species in fear they would overtake human society. As long as he'd lived, he'd never found reason to question their opinions. It changed one day when he came across a member of the species he'd been meant to kill. Unlike the usual violent reaction he'd witnessed, the person instead begged him for help. Vague found himself curious. Over time, Vague befriends Descent—the person he assisted. Descent takes refuge hidden in Vague's house, and the two of them slowly uncover violent secrets their societies had been hiding from them. The two of them don't find peace in this, however. While Vague is already grappling with the knowledge he's learning from Descent, a band of radicals is trying to recruit him as well. Time is quickly running out as Vague tries to decide which side to take.

Art & Design

Allegories takes a more earthy approach to design. The primary setting is a town deep within a grassland. Right now, my designs need to be revamped and redone more cohesively in terms of the environment and resources. The designs presented are the version 1 designs, which were created before a majority of the story was created.

Character Design

Shiem

Shiem's original design followed a more traditionally alternative aesthetic. In his updated design, I wanted his clothes to be more visibly similar to Tea's, who makes them. Shiem is meant to stand out from other characters in terms of seeming a lot more sure of himself. His clothes are overall brighter and more accessorised to try and convey this. While other characters are internally confident, I wanted a character who looks confident even though they're not. His iridescent shirt beneath the jacket also reflects his place in the story: One of the most reliable members of Vague's society (in his age group), secretly hiding a relationship with an alien underneath.

Costa

Costa revolves around neutrals, greens, and overall outfits which make him easy to pass over. His outfits provide internal pockets and loose shapes for him to hide supplies in, but also reflect his little sense of self. He's not expected to stand out, so he doesn't, even though he's incredibly skilled—that's what I needed his design to portray.

Nox

Nox's outfit and overall design is incredibly detailed, but nonsensical. Their original design was based on webcore and tenshi kaiwai aesthetics, but later was changed to a more traditionally cyberpunk aesthetic. Their current outfit reflects aspects of the original designs, but was changed to reflect the available resources and dyes in their environment. I kept the blues and pops of colour, as well as the cutouts and purification mask (which was optimised and given to everyone else as well). They still stand out, and are meant to look odd compared to everyone else, so I'm happy with their current design.

Colorways and Aesthetic

Allegories in total typically has a mix of pale, desaturated colours paired with extremely oversaturated ones. The colour palette shares the same fight as the characters do; most people are meant to have some very contradictory colours to represent that. Others who have very little sense of self and feel like they're getting swept away in the battle (such as Costa and Vague) display little contrast. The art I provide doesn't match the usual theme, because the characters I draw are usually a part of climatic elements of the story. In turn, most of my associated art with Allegories is heavily oversaturated. Alongside this, I make sure to add a prevailing theme of overshadowing darkness in the palette.

Versions

Nox 1.0, 1.5, 2.0

Nox's transition from webcore to cyberpunk was a big one. I knew that I wanted Nox to have a bigger presence, which is why I changed their more tame initial design. Nox's main focus is on catching attention and being the most eye-catching person in their environment, and that lead to me designing an overall dark outfit with bright highlights. I decided to keep a similar idea by having bright pops of yellow and other colours in their revised outfit. I also wanted to properly portray their inability to dress cohesively, which I decided to cover by adding unusual layering and color combinations.

Iocom 1.0, 1.5, 2.0

Iocom's design was a hard project to overcome. I had originally wanted him to have strong ethnic features, leading me to heavily reference some traits found in Masatoshi Hamada's face (a popular Japanese comedian whom I grew up watching). However, I ended up overgeneralizing these features and creating a design I was very unsatisfied with. I knew for a fact I didn't want him to look like a stereotypical Chinese mob boss, since depictions of that nature are inherently incredibly racist. I had initially wanted his child, Nox, to have two Chinese parents. However after this design, I settled on having two heavily mixed parents with prevailing Chinese ancestry. I then played with this to create a mixed white/asian design for Iocom. I absolutely hated this. He didn't represent his POC culture enough in this design, and he was also far too red-based for my liking. Later, I revisited him and decided to combine the features I enjoyed from both drawings. I wanted to keep a more diamond-shaped face seen in his initial design, and I also liked the bob-like hair seen in his second design. I wanted him to appear softer, but still commanding. His second hair style was very dear to me, but I didn't like how it framed his face, so I decided to add in braids. From there, I decided to add in more AAPI traits. Overall, he ended up with mixed Chinese/Hawaiian/Greek heritage. I'm very happy with his current design.

Things I've Learnt

As I've contributed to Allegories, I've learnt how having small things that set some characters apart from others is important. The difference between colours on a simple outfit can change the entire portrayal of a character. I've also learnt how to translate character traits from one style to another. My work on this project paved the road of my lineart's maturation, but with that comes the need to adjust initial designs to a new style. I particularly saw this problem with Jhim, who had overly stylised eyes. Overall, this project was very important to my development of lineart and understanding of cohesive character/environment design.
