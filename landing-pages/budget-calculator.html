Budgeting Calculator


https://eecubudgeting-icarus.netlify.app/

Late Feb 2024 - Mid Mar 2024



Intro

This project was a collaboration with the EECU, which gave budgeting presentations to our class just a few days before this was assigned. We were tasked with creating a budgeting site based off of concepts we researched online. This was also my first time independently utilising JavaScript within a site. Besides this, my classmates and I were directed to have others code our site for us. During this project, I not only learnt how to communicate from a managerial stance, but also acquired experience coding for others as well.

Prototyping

After completing a competitive audit on 5 sites, the thing I was trying to avoid the most was having my design look like how the DMV feels. I focused on having a simple user-friendly design with appropriate, fresh-looking colors and a business-friendly look. While my site was originally planned to be two pages, my understanding of JavaScript was too rudimentary to properly implement some of the features I wanted. I adapted my site's needs to my own capabilities as best I could, but was unable to discern how to make a key part of my site (chart.js) function properly.


Colorways and Aesthetic




As I researched competitor designs for budgeting sites, I found a common color scheme. Most sites tended to use some combination of dark greens, blues, greys, or white. However, two sites (Voya and The Balance Money) had very unexpected palettes. The unique colorways of these sites inspired me, and I enjoyed how they appeared more friendly to the eye. Originally, I intended on using colours similar to Voya's choices. However, in the end, I recalled how a palette can truly make or break a site such as in my experience creating Jurassic Expeditions. I fell back on safer green/blue tones, but lightened them to be less threatening.


Final Site


My final design remained very true to my original prototype, though I left out some features like seeing the change between your previous budgeting to your current. Overall, I'm somewhat proud of how it turned out. If I was to return to this project, I would add back in those features and make the pie chart functional.


Result Quality


Success


This project was an overall improvement from my first interactable site, Jurassic Expeditions. The design of my prototype translated over almost seamlessly into code. I also learnt how to properly communicate with “employees” about what I was looking for. This project may not have turned out perfectly, but it was nonetheless a valued learning experience for JavaScript implementation and teamwork.

Failure


The failures of this site mostly reside within the capabilities of my JavaScript. I was unable to display many values correctly. I also had issues with properly styling displays at the end of the site as well as the labelling for the chart/s.


Takeaways


Through this project, I better acquainted myself with JavaScript. Proper user interface also came to me more clearly as I was designing this. I believe this site was the catalyst which helped a lot of aspects within the coding and UX world click for me.



Extra + BTS

  • For two-three weeks this project was completely coded by classmates following either my word-for-word directions or going off my Figma prototype.

  • I spent an additional 4-5 days (in total) polishing the site by myself. This total span of time took place off and on over the course of 2-3 more weeks.
