Myopic

Culture & Society Development

Early Jan 2024 - Current


A collection of the initial sketches of the main trio. I was aiming to have their clothes reflect cyberpunk elements, but also look familiar, which I obtained by through a combination of common cotton texturing and glossy material.

Intro

Myopic is a total worldbuilding project which is very early in production. This encompasses the creation of an entire society, culture, laws, language, and a few characters. Currently, the main focus is on culture design.

Story Outline

The cities of Berevi and Sul are one of many which were erected during the final years of Earth's time supporting the human race. After the drastic effects of climate change, many billionaires paired with scientists and engineers to create a new society elsewhere in the universe. Realising the opportunity they had with this project, they created a new language and did their best to erase what traditional culture they could. Through this was birthed the Sinst cities. These cities have no interaction with each other and are located in massive domes. Inside these cities, many different forms of society have developed.

Character Design

Rexhe

Rexhe's (pronounced reh-t-jey) design consists mainly of streetwear, and was the first outfit I designed for this project. His outfit is meant to be eye-catching, bright, and a bit mismatched. Designing this outfit was one of the first times I focused on using bright pops of colour on top of a light neutral base instead of a dark one. It was also my first time willingly incorporating patterns into my art, which I typically heavily avoid. These can be seen within his hair and also on his arms as he ages.

Ghatku

Ghatku's (pronounced gah-tsuh) design relies on earthy colours and natural dyes, since that's all his society has access to. His design was the first where I actually needed to consider the available materials, dyes, and necessary functionality. His clothes are also all lined with cinches so as to close when he accesses the inverted city. The nuances that are found in outfits due to environment and available materials is very important to consider, which designing for him taught me.

Oyil

The clothes seen on Oyil are purposely meant to mismatched while having a form of cohesive style. In his major design, Oyil is wearing clothes which don't belong to him. It's important to consider how other character's clothes fit on him and how he manages to style it. This was an important exercise for me since I had to get creative with the realities of how outfits don't always perfectly fit who they're wearing.


Colorways and Aesthetic




So far, Myopic doesn't have an overall colorway or aesthetic as I've hardly drawn anything for it. However, there are stark differences between Berevi and Sul colorways which will be seen when they are eventually drawn. Berevi has more resources and technology, leading to more availability of brighter colours. Neons, greys, blacks, and other hard-to-find colours are seen more in Berevian designs (such as Rexhe and Oyil's). In contrast to Berevi, Sul relies mostly on natural dyes produced from food products and other natural resources. Sul itself is far more brown-based and less saturated overall. The more saturated a colour is, the darker it will get. I've learned that just these colour choices can show a lot about the differences in a society.


Versions

Ghatku 0.5, 1.0, 2.0


Ghatku's initial design was created before any world development on Sul was made. Initially, it was planned to be a society similar to Berevi in terms of resource availability. As development went on, it turned out that their fabric choices needed to be wildly different, which meant Ghatku's current outfit was out of the question. As I redesigned him, I wanted to adapt parts of his sharp aesthetic into his new outfit. Obviously, with loose fabrics, it can't directly transfer over. Instead, I utilised proportions and kept his top short. I also included leg wrappings and dark, saturated colours. While it doesn't directly reflect his initial design, I felt the overall vibe of what I'd been trying to get across translated nicely.



Things I've Learnt


This project greatly expanded my horizons on how character design and worldbuilding goes together; especially that outfit design is affected by the world around it. Before working on this project, I'd designed characters and left them unchanged, or put them in outfits which didn't match resource availability. Errors like this can be seen in my Allegories design of Nox. Being constrained by these things not only made my characters make more sense in their world, but also taught me to get creative with the functionality of clothing.
